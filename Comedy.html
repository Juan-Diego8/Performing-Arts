Comedy

Definition

Comedy is a genre whose plot seeks to make the public laugh, whether through ironies, parodies, confusion, mistakes or sarcasm. The word comedy derives from the Greek komoidía, a word composed of komos that indicates a parade and odé that refers to a song or ode. In this sense, the classical comedy of Greek theater consisted of burlesque songs and political satires, also known as satirical poems.

how it begins?

This genre take its place in Ancient Greece and is part of classical theater, which initially only comprised tragedy.

Examples

Some of the main works that represent this genre are: Lysistrata.,The bees, Clouds,Peace,The Frogs,The Acarnians,The Knights and The birds.
