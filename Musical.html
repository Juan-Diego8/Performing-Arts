Musical

Definition

Musical theatre is a form of theatrical performance that combines songs, spoken dialogue, acting and dance. Its origin is found in various European theatrical sources of the 19th century, known as operetta, comic opera, musical comedy or French vaudeville. They were variety shows without dramatic unity that included popular songs, dancers, acrobats, and comic numbers.
