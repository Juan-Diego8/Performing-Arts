Tragedy

Definition

Tragedy is a dramatic genre characterized by the representation of serious and elevated themes that lead its protagonist to a fateful outcome. Tragedy, as a theatrical genre, is a literary expression that originates from Ancient Greece. As such, it was composed in verse and in a high tone, representing the story of a character who, by passion or by fatality, was led to his downfall (death or moral destruction) of him.
